THE IMPORTANCE OF PLAY

Over screentime.

"Children are spending almost an hour per day in front of a screen before they turn one."

—Dr.Leigh Tooth, Screen time no child's play

Dr Tooth's study showed screen time quickly increased with age before plateauing around three years, at an average of 94 minutes per weekday.

Screen time represents a missed opportunity where children could be practising and mastering a developmental skill, like skipping and jumping, over being sedentary and transfixed to a screen.

It's very easy to use screen time with children because there are so many child-friendly apps and games developed for young children and parents. monitoring screen time in occordance with the can protect your baby's health and development.

World Health Organization Guidelines

Children 1–2 years of age:

  • For 1-year-olds, sedentary screen time (such as watching TV or videos, playing computer games) is not recommended. For those aged 2 years, sedentary screen time should be no more than 1 hour; less is better.

Suggested activities:

When sedentary, engaging in reading and storytelling with a caregiver is encouraged.

Children 3–4 years of age:

  • Not be restrained for more than 1 hour at a time Sedentary screen time should be no more than 1 hour; less is better.

Suggested activities:

When sedentary, engaging in reading and storytelling with a caregiver is encouraged.
